About Campus Explorer

Campus Explorer is an interactive induction game designed for prospective university students who want to familiarize themselves with their future campus environment. Whether you missed the open day, want additional information, or simply prefer exploring at your own pace, Campus Explorer offers an engaging way to learn about university facilities from anywhere, anytime.

Navigate through an accurate representation of the university campus, interact with buildings to learn about different departments, collect coins for rewards, and discover all the university has to offer in this interactive experience!

Accurate Campus Map

Explore a detailed top-down map of the university with accurate building layouts, pathways, trees, and grass areas designed to match the real campus environment.

Interactive Learning

Interact with buildings and signposts across the campus to receive valuable information about different areas, helping you understand what happens in each facility.

Explore & Collect

Find and collect coins scattered throughout the campus that can be spent in the in-game shop on cosmetic items, access to special areas, and other rewards.

Navigation Assistance

Use the mini-map feature to easily track your location and find your way around the campus, just like you would in real life.

Immersive Experience

Enjoy background music and sound effects that enhance your virtual campus tour experience, making exploration more engaging and fun.

Accessible Anywhere

Access the game on your computer or through a web browser, allowing you to explore your future university whenever and wherever you want.

Why Campus Explorer?

Campus Explorer was developed to solve a common problem: many students apply to universities without being familiar with the campus environment. While open days and virtual tours are available, they aren't always accessible to everyone.

Our game provides an interactive and informative alternative, allowing prospective students to learn about the university at their own pace. It's not meant to replace in-person visits but to supplement them, giving you a head start in understanding your future academic home.
